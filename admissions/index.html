Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to what every student in Samarkand asks about university admissions.

Do I need a high SAT score to apply to US universities?
Many US universities are now test-optional, but a strong SAT score (1350+) significantly improves your chances and qualifies you for merit scholarships worth thousands of dollars. We always recommend preparing for and submitting the SAT.
How early should I start the admissions process?
Ideally 12–18 months before your intended enrollment date. If you want to start university in September 2025, you should begin in early 2024. Starting late is the #1 reason students miss deadlines and scholarship opportunities.
Can I get scholarships to US universities from Uzbekistan?
Yes. Many US universities offer generous merit scholarships to international students — some covering 50–100% of tuition. Our students have received scholarships worth $15,000–$40,000 per year. The key is applying to the right schools with a strong application.
What is Common App and do I need it?
Common App is the main application platform used by 1,000+ US universities. Most selective US schools use it. We guide you through every section: activities list, essays, recommendations, and submission. You only fill it out once for multiple schools.
How does the student visa process work for Uzbekistan?
Once accepted, the university sends you an I-20 form. You then complete the DS-160 form, pay the SEVIS fee, and attend a visa interview at the US Embassy in Tashkent. We prepare you for every step, including the interview questions.
Are your consultants real university graduates or just tutors?
Our founder and mentors have personally gone through competitive university applications. Every mentor scored 1500+ on the SAT and most have been through the US or European university admissions process themselves. We teach from lived experience.
