FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to what every student asks about university admissions.

Do I need a high SAT score to apply to US universities?

Many US universities are now test-optional, but a strong SAT score (1350+) significantly improves your chances and qualifies you for merit scholarships worth thousands of dollars.

How early should I start the admissions process?

Ideally 12–18 months before your intended enrollment date. Starting late is the #1 reason students miss deadlines and scholarship opportunities.

Can I get scholarships to US universities from Uzbekistan?

Yes. Many US universities offer generous merit scholarships — some covering 50–100% of tuition. Our students have received $15,000–$40,000 per year.

What is Common App and do I need it?

Common App is used by 1,000+ US universities. You fill it out once for multiple schools — we guide every section.

How does the student visa process work?

Once accepted, the university sends an I-20. You complete DS-160, pay SEVIS fee, and attend a visa interview in Tashkent — we prepare you for every step.

Are your consultants real university graduates?

Our founder and mentors personally went through competitive university applications. Every mentor earned a top SAT score.
